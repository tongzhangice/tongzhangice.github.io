Who am I?

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in the Fluid Dynamics and Sold Mechanics Group (T-3) of Los Alamos National Laboratory, New Mexico, United States.

My research interest mainly lies in the fields of ice dynamics and its interaction with climate and ocean circulation. In particular, I am interested in the thermo-mechanical behaviors of (marine) ice sheet, e.g., the grounding line dynamics, and also the dynamical changes of alpine glaciers in Tibet.

Before I came to New Mexico, I am an Assistant Professor of the Institute of Polar Meteorology, Chinese Academy of Meteorological Sciences (Dec 2015 – Nov 2017). Before that, I did two-year postdoctoral work in the Department of Mathematics & Interdisciplinary Mathematical Institute, University of South Carolina, USA (Aug 2013 – Aug 2015)

I was born in a small town in Zhejiang Province, southeast of China 33 years ago. It’s a beautiful town that has many historical and natural scenes. I have a 4-year old son named Michael. My wife is from Hebei Province, north of China. We currently enjoy our lives vey much in New Mexio.
