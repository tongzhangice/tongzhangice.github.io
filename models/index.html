Ice flow models

The current model I am working at is called MALI (Mpas-Albany Land Ice model), a cooperative product between Los Alamos National Laboratory and Sandia National Laboratories. It is based on the classic first-order Blatter-Pattyn approximation. It’s a land ice component of MPAS.

I was also working on a full-Stokes ice sheet model when I was in University of South Carolina. The model is called FELIX-S which is mostly written in C. FELIX-S and was developed in the framework of PHG, a finite-element framework from Chinese Academy of Sciences.

The first model I developed and used was a two dimensional flow-band first-order ice-flow model. It’s also thermo-mechanically coupled. It is good for understanding the dynamics of alpine glaciers.
