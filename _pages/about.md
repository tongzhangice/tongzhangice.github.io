I am an ice flow modeler that seeks to combine cutting-edge technologies and land ice simulations in the climate system, including:

The physical mechanisms of land ice within the Earth's climate system and its interactions with the atmosphere and ocean; the instability of the Antarctic and Greenland ice sheets and their impacts on sea-level rise; glacial changes, water resource variability, and glacier-related hazards on the Tibetan Plateau; interdisciplinary integration of physical modeling, artificial intelligence, and remote sensing big data; developer for two-dimensional and three-dimensional land ice dynamics models, along with multiple field expeditions to the Earth’s "three poles" (Antarctica, the Arctic, and the Tibetan Plateau). Full participation in all four Antarctic ice sheet model intercomparison projects (initMIP, LARMIP2, ABUMIP, ISMIP6) under the IPCC AR6 framework.
